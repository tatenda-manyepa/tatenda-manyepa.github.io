Modernisation Objective

The modernisation phase builds on the migration foundation by improving how the platform is deployed, monitored, secured, recovered and operated. Rather than simply moving workloads to AWS, this phase focuses on improving architecture quality, reducing manual operational effort, increasing release confidence, and creating the technical foundation for intelligent operations.

Refactor Selectively

Modernise components where refactoring delivers clear resilience, scalability, maintainability or deployment benefits.

Improve Platform Reliability

Introduce resilience patterns, health checks, recovery automation and operational safeguards.

Reduce Manual Operations

Automate repetitive tasks such as health checks, deployment validation, rollback support and operational alerts.

Strengthen Observability

Implement centralised metrics, logs, dashboards, alerts and operational visibility across the platform.

Secure Operational Access

Improve IAM, secrets management, access governance, audit logging and least-privilege operational controls.

Enable AI Operations

Prepare logs, runbooks, incidents and documentation for future AI-assisted troubleshooting and root cause analysis.

Modernisation Workstreams

Application Refactoring

Split tightly coupled components where appropriate, introduce APIs, improve service boundaries and prepare workloads for containerisation.

CI/CD Pipeline

Introduce controlled build, test, approval, deployment, validation and rollback processes for safer change delivery.

Observability Platform

Standardise logging, metrics, dashboards, alarms and operational health reporting across infrastructure and application layers.

Reliability Engineering

Define health checks, failure scenarios, recovery patterns, RTO/RPO targets and resilience testing practices.

Secrets & Access Management

Introduce secure storage, rotation, auditability and access control for operational credentials and sensitive configuration.

Runbooks & Operational Standards

Create standardised troubleshooting guides, escalation paths, recovery procedures and post-incident review practices.

CI/CD & Controlled Hotfix Deployment

Modernisation introduces safer release processes so that hotfixes and operational changes can be validated, approved, deployed and rolled back with greater confidence.

1. Source Control

Application code, infrastructure scripts, configuration templates and documentation are version controlled.

2. Automated Validation

Build checks, linting, unit tests, configuration validation and security scanning run before deployment.

3. Environment Promotion

Changes move through development, test, staging and production environments with approval gates.

4. Deployment Strategy

Blue/green, rolling or controlled deployment strategies reduce release risk and support rollback.

5. Post-Deployment Monitoring

CloudWatch alarms, logs, dashboards and incident workflows validate whether the release is stable.

6. Rollback & RCA

Failed releases trigger rollback procedures, incident review and architecture or process improvements.

Observability & Support Modernisation

Centralised Dashboards

Provide visibility into application health, infrastructure performance, database metrics, queue depth and fulfilment workflows.

Proactive Alerting

Detect service degradation, storage pressure, database timeouts, queue delays and failed workflows before major impact.

Log Correlation

Correlate logs across application, database, messaging and infrastructure layers to reduce investigation time.

Auto-Ticketing Foundation

Prepare alerts and incident metadata so tickets can be created with affected services, symptoms and suggested actions.

Runbook Integration

Connect alerts to standardised runbooks so support teams can follow consistent investigation and recovery steps.

Operational Insights

Use recurring incident patterns to identify reliability improvements and future automation opportunities.

Security & Governance Modernisation

Apply role-based access and least privilege to reduce unnecessary administrative permissions.

Secrets Management

Move sensitive credentials into managed secrets storage with rotation and auditability.

Audit Logging

Use CloudTrail and operational logs to support investigation, compliance and accountability.

Tagging & Ownership

Apply tagging standards for cost tracking, ownership, environment separation and operational governance.

Policy Guardrails

Define guardrails around access, networking, backups, encryption, logging and change control.

Operational Accountability

Clarify service ownership, escalation paths, access responsibilities and review processes.

Modernisation Risks & Controls

Over-Refactoring

Risk: Refactoring too much too early increases delivery risk.
Control: Prioritise components with clear business value and operational benefit.

Release Instability

Risk: Faster deployments can create production issues.
Control: Automated tests, approvals, rollback and post-release monitoring.

Tooling Complexity

Risk: Too many tools increase operational burden.
Control: Standardise tooling and keep operational workflows simple.

Security Drift

Risk: Permissions and secrets become unmanaged over time.
Control: IAM reviews, rotation policies and audit logging.

Alert Fatigue

Risk: Too many alerts reduce trust in monitoring.
Control: Tune thresholds, severity levels and actionable alert design.

Support Adoption

Risk: Teams do not adopt new runbooks or dashboards.
Control: Training, documentation, hypercare and feedback loops.
