Current Environment Overview

The organisation operates a predominantly on-premises fulfilment environment made up of virtualised application servers, Microsoft SQL Server databases, messaging services, fulfilment management systems, reporting, print services, and robotic fulfilment technologies. These systems collectively support inventory allocation, order processing, fulfilment execution, shipping preparation, and operational reporting.

Core Platform Components

Application Services

Order fulfilment applications and operational services supporting fulfilment workflows and user activity.

Database Services

Microsoft SQL Server environment supporting operational workloads, transactions, reporting and fulfilment data.

Messaging Services

Message queue infrastructure supporting system communication between fulfilment applications and dependent services.

Robotic Fulfilment Systems

Automated inventory movement and fulfilment execution systems supporting operational throughput.

Print Services

Operational label and shipping documentation generation required for dispatch and fulfilment completion.

Reporting Services

Operational and management reporting used to understand fulfilment performance and operational activity.

Key Integration Dependencies

The current platform relies on several critical integration points. Any disruption to these dependencies can directly impact fulfilment operations.

Fulfilment Platform ↔ Database

Database connectivity issues can delay order processing, inventory updates and fulfilment execution.

Fulfilment Platform ↔ Messaging

Messaging delays or blocked queues can disrupt workflow coordination and system communication.

Fulfilment Platform ↔ Robotics

Communication failures with robotic fulfilment systems can stop automated movement and picking workflows.

Inventory ↔ Reporting

Reporting depends on accurate inventory and fulfilment data to support operational decision-making.

Applications ↔ Print Services

Printing failures can delay labelling, dispatch preparation and fulfilment completion.

Support ↔ Knowledge Sources

Support teams depend on individual knowledge, historic incidents and fragmented documentation during investigation.

Infrastructure & Operational Risks

Manual Failover

Recovery activities depend heavily on support personnel, increasing recovery time and the risk of human error.

Storage Capacity Pressure

Application and database storage volumes periodically approach capacity limits, increasing instability and operational risk.

Database Performance Limitations

Long-running queries and inefficient processing can reduce application responsiveness and fulfilment throughput.

Service Recovery Issues

Following patching and maintenance, critical services do not always restart successfully without manual intervention.

Integration Communication Failures

Failures between applications, databases, messaging services and robotic systems can disrupt fulfilment operations.

Immature DR Capability

Disaster recovery is reactive, with limited recovery automation, unclear objectives and limited formal testing.

Support & Operational Maturity

Reactive Support Model

Operational issues are typically identified after services have already degraded or failed. Support teams spend significant effort reacting to incidents rather than proactively preventing them.

Knowledge Management Challenges

Operational knowledge is heavily dependent on individual team members and institutional experience, creating inconsistent troubleshooting and knowledge transfer difficulties.

Manual Operational Processes

Activities such as failover, service recovery, health checks and investigation are heavily manual and depend on support intervention.

Limited Observability

Current monitoring supports reactive investigation rather than proactive operational management, predictive detection or centralised visibility.

Transformation Opportunities

The current-state assessment identifies clear opportunities for cloud modernisation, improved operational maturity, stronger resilience, better observability and AI-assisted support transformation.

Cloud Migration

Migrate core workloads to an AWS-first architecture with improved scalability, resilience and operational flexibility.

Observability Platform

Introduce centralised monitoring, logging, dashboards, alerting and operational intelligence.

DR & Resilience

Define RTO/RPO targets, automate backups, validate recovery and introduce documented recovery runbooks.

Automation & CI/CD

Improve release safety, validation, rollback and hotfix deployment through controlled automation.

Security & Access Governance

Improve IAM, secrets management, auditability, privileged access control and operational accountability.

AI-Assisted Operations

Use AI to support troubleshooting, knowledge retrieval, incident correlation and root cause analysis.
