Current-state architecture for a legacy enterprise fulfilment environment with tightly coupled systems, manual recovery processes, limited observability, and reactive operational support.

Architecture Overview

This diagram shows the current operational platform flow from external customer systems through the fulfilment platform, database, messaging, robot control system, robotic fleet, reporting services, application logging, and manual support model.

The diagram is intentionally modelled as a current-state view rather than an ideal architecture. It highlights the operational reality: fulfilment workflows depend on tightly coupled systems, manual recovery, fragmented investigation paths, primary/standby infrastructure, and site-reported incidents.

Key Current-State Risks

These risks explain why the platform requires modernisation. They map directly to the affected architecture layers and form the basis for the AWS target-state design.

🔴 SQL Performance Bottlenecks

Affected Layer: SQL Operational Database

Long-running queries, timeouts and reporting failures impact operational visibility and fulfilment processes.

🔴 Allocation Lock Exceptions

Affected Layer: Order Fulfilment Platform

Database locking can interrupt stock allocation and order processing workflows.

🔴 Messaging Queue Backlogs

Affected Layer: Messaging Layer

Delayed processing within the messaging layer can impact robotic fulfilment and system-to-system communication.

🔴 Drive Capacity Exhaustion

Affected Layer: Database & Log Storage

Database and log storage growth contribute to performance degradation and service instability.

🔴 Robot Task Idling

Affected Layer: Robot Control System

Network and application communication issues can leave robotic tasks incomplete or waiting indefinitely.

🔴 No Proactive Monitoring

Affected Layer: Operational Support Model

Operational failures are often identified by site personnel rather than automated alerting and observability tooling.

🔴 Manual Failover & Recovery

Affected Layer: Availability & Recovery

Recovery activities rely heavily on support intervention and individual operational knowledge.

🔴 Manual Patching & Hotfix Deployment

Affected Layer: Release & Change Management

Production changes require manual coordination, increasing deployment risk and recovery times.

🔴 No CI/CD Release Pipeline

Affected Layer: Software Delivery

Software deployment, validation and rollback processes are not automated or standardised.

Business Impact

The risks above create measurable business impact across fulfilment operations, customer delivery, support workload and platform reliability.

Delayed Customer Deliveries

Fulfilment disruption can delay order picking, shipping preparation, and customer delivery timelines.

Reduced Operational Throughput

Database, messaging, robotic control, and network issues can reduce the volume of orders processed.

Increased Downtime Cost

Manual recovery and unclear failover processes extend service disruption during high-severity incidents.

High Support Burden

Support teams spend significant time investigating recurring issues instead of improving platform resilience.

Slow Incident Resolution

Fragmented logs, limited alerting and knowledge silos slow down root cause analysis.

Operational Risk During Peak Periods

Scalability constraints, database bottlenecks and manual processes increase risk during high-volume periods.

Why Transformation Is Required

The current architecture supports fulfilment operations, but its resilience and operational maturity are limited by legacy infrastructure, manual recovery processes, reactive support, and limited observability.

Move from Reactive to Proactive Operations

Introduce centralised monitoring, alerting, dashboards, log correlation, and operational intelligence.

Improve Resilience & Recovery

Reduce manual failover dependency with better backup, recovery, availability, and disaster recovery design.

Modernise Infrastructure

Move toward scalable cloud infrastructure with improved capacity management and reduced server maintenance overhead.

Enable AI-Assisted Support

Use AI to retrieve knowledge, correlate incidents, summarise logs, suggest runbooks, and support faster investigations.
