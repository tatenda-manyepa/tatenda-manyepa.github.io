Design Goals

The platform is designed to move support from reactive, experience-dependent troubleshooting toward structured, AI-assisted investigation. The goal is to help engineers find evidence faster, understand system behaviour, reuse operational knowledge and produce better post-incident learning.

Accelerate Investigations

Bring together alerts, logs, tickets, documentation and release context so engineers can understand incidents faster.

Improve System Understanding

Use AI to explain application workflows, dependencies, known issues and operational behaviour during investigations.

Guide Consistent Troubleshooting

Suggest structured investigation steps, validation checks, escalation routes and rollback considerations.

Support RCA Creation

Help engineers draft evidence-based RCA summaries after ticket investigation, including timeline, cause and prevention actions.

Capture Operational Learning

Turn resolved incidents, approved RCAs and updated runbooks into reusable knowledge for future support activity.

Maintain Human Accountability

Keep engineers responsible for validation, production actions, customer updates and final RCA approval.

AI-Assisted Incident Investigation Workflow

This workflow demonstrates how operational telemetry, historical support knowledge, engineering documentation and AI-assisted reasoning work together to support engineers throughout an incident investigation. Rather than replacing engineers, AI accelerates investigation, suggests investigation steps, retrieves system context, assists with Root Cause Analysis (RCA), and helps improve organisational knowledge after every incident.

Human-in-the-loop principle: AI supports the investigation by retrieving evidence, identifying similar incidents, explaining system context, suggesting investigation paths and helping draft RCA outputs. Engineers remain responsible for validating evidence, making operational decisions, applying fixes, escalating where required and approving customer-facing updates.

Workflow Explained

1. Incident Detection

Incidents may originate from CloudWatch alarms, automated monitoring, or customer-reported support tickets. Each event enters a shared investigation workflow so alerts and user-reported issues can be handled consistently.

2. Operational Intelligence

The Operational Intelligence Service retrieves relevant evidence from CloudWatch metrics and logs, OpenSearch, historical tickets, system documentation, runbooks, release notes, hot fixes, rollback history and project changes.

3. AI-Assisted Investigation

AI suggests investigation steps, explains application workflows, retrieves relevant documentation, identifies similar incidents and recommends next actions based on trusted operational knowledge.

4. Engineer Validation

The support engineer performs the investigation using AI guidance, validates evidence, checks system behaviour and decides whether to resolve, rollback, escalate or continue investigating.

5. AI-Assisted RCA

After the ticket investigation, AI helps draft the RCA by summarising the timeline, symptoms, impact, root cause, contributing factors, resolution steps and prevention actions for engineer review.

6. Validated Learning Loop

Approved RCAs, updated runbooks, known issues, release notes and architecture changes are written back into the operational knowledge base so future investigations become faster and more accurate.

Enterprise Knowledge Platform

The AI assistant is only useful when it is grounded in trusted operational, engineering and support knowledge. These sources form the Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) foundation for investigation guidance.

Operational Data

CloudWatch metrics, application logs, infrastructure health, queue behaviour, database performance and service events.

Engineering Knowledge

Architecture documentation, ADRs, system diagrams, runbooks, SOPs, support procedures and recovery guidance.

Delivery Knowledge

Azure DevOps release notes, deployment history, software changes, hot fixes, rollback history and known product defects.

Operational History

Historic tickets, customer impact notes, previous fixes, recurring incident patterns, RCA summaries and lessons learned.

Search & Retrieval

OpenSearch and vector retrieval help surface relevant evidence, similar incidents and documentation during investigations.

Validated Updates

Only approved RCA outputs, runbook updates and engineer-reviewed knowledge are fed back into the platform.

AI Governance Principles

Human-in-the-Loop

AI suggests actions and evidence, but engineers remain responsible for validation, decisions and production changes.

Data Privacy

Operational data, logs, tickets and customer information require careful access control, masking and governance.

Trustworthy Sources

Responses should be grounded in approved runbooks, architecture documents and operational knowledge sources.

Explainability

AI recommendations should show why a suggestion was made and which sources informed the response.

Change Control

AI should not directly deploy fixes without approval gates, validation checks and rollback controls.

Continuous Review

AI outputs, runbooks, incident summaries and recommendations should be reviewed and improved over time.

Future AWS Implementation Roadmap

The current portfolio page presents the target design and workflow. The implementation can be delivered incrementally so each phase produces visible evidence in GitHub and AWS.

Phase 1: Knowledge Ingestion

Ingest architecture docs, runbooks, support notes, release notes, known issues and RCA examples into an S3-backed knowledge store.

Phase 2: RAG Retrieval

Use embeddings, OpenSearch vector search and Bedrock Knowledge Base patterns to retrieve relevant operational context.

Phase 3: Operational Integrations

Connect CloudWatch, EventBridge and ticketing workflows so alerts and customer tickets can trigger structured investigation context.

Phase 4: AI Copilot

Build an engineer-facing assistant that suggests investigation steps, explains system context and drafts RCA outputs.

Phase 5: Feedback Controls

Capture engineer validation, rejected suggestions and approved RCA learning before updating the operational knowledge base.

Phase 6: Reporting & Improvement

Track recurring issues, RCA themes, knowledge gaps, support pressure and opportunities for automation or architecture change.
