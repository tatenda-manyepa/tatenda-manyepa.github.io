Purpose of the Assessment Phase

The assessment phase establishes why the transformation is required, what risks exist in the current environment, which systems and operational processes are affected, and what outcomes the target architecture must achieve.

Business Context

Understand the organisation, operational model, business drivers, customer impact and transformation objectives.

Current-State Review

Assess infrastructure, applications, databases, messaging, reporting, monitoring and support processes.

Risk Identification

Identify service outages, manual recovery, observability gaps, integration failures and DR weaknesses.

Dependency Mapping

Map relationships between fulfilment applications, databases, messaging, robotics, reporting and printing services.

Operational Maturity

Evaluate support model, knowledge management, manual processes and troubleshooting consistency.

Transformation Scope

Define the programme scope for cloud migration, observability, security, resilience and AI-assisted operations.

Assessment Findings

The current environment supports day-to-day operations but exhibits several limitations that increase operational risk, reduce resilience and limit scalability.

Recurring Service Outages

Operational outages affect inventory allocation, order processing and fulfilment execution.

Manual Failover

Failover and recovery rely heavily on manual support intervention and specialist knowledge.

Infrastructure Constraints

Storage capacity, maintenance overhead and limited scalability create operational pressure.

Database Performance

Long-running queries and inefficient processing affect application responsiveness and throughput.

Messaging Reliability

Delayed or blocked message processing disrupts workflow execution and system communication.

Immature DR Posture

Disaster recovery capability is limited, reactive and not consistently validated through formal testing.

Business Impact

Delayed Customer Deliveries

Service disruption impacts order fulfilment and customer delivery timelines.

Reduced Throughput

Operational bottlenecks reduce fulfilment efficiency and platform productivity.

Increased Support Pressure

Reactive support increases workload, stress and dependency on individual engineers.

Higher Operational Cost

Downtime, emergency maintenance and manual intervention increase operating cost.

Business Continuity Risk

Weak DR capability increases the risk of extended service interruption during major incidents.

Reduced Confidence

Recurring instability reduces stakeholder confidence in platform reliability and scalability.

Transformation Drivers

Improve Resilience

Reduce recurring outages and improve availability across fulfilment operations.

Strengthen DR

Define recovery objectives, improve backup governance and validate recovery procedures.

Improve Observability

Introduce centralised monitoring, dashboards, alerts and operational visibility.

Enable Scalability

Support future business growth and peak operational periods through cloud scalability.

Reduce Manual Effort

Automate repetitive support activities, recovery steps and operational checks.

Enable AI Operations

Prepare the platform for AI-assisted troubleshooting, knowledge retrieval and guided incident response.

Assessment Methodology

1. Business Context Review

Understand operational environment, fulfilment processes, user impact, business drivers and transformation objectives.

2. Current-State Discovery

Review application landscape, infrastructure, databases, messaging, reporting, printing and robotic fulfilment dependencies.

3. Pain Point Analysis

Identify recurring incidents, service instability, bottlenecks, manual processes and operational maturity gaps.

4. Risk Assessment

Assess business and technical impact of service outages, manual failover, storage constraints, database limitations and weak DR.

5. Transformation Scope Definition

Define the target areas for migration, resilience, observability, automation, governance and AI-assisted operations.

Assessment Outputs

Business Case Study

Executive business context, drivers, objectives, scope and success criteria.

Current-State Architecture

Interactive current-state platform view showing dependencies, risks and modernisation opportunities.

Target Architecture Input

Assessment findings directly inform the AWS-first target-state architecture and migration priorities.

Migration Strategy Input

Current-state risks and dependencies guide migration waves, rollback plans, validation and cutover planning.
